# Al-Fatiha — LearnQuran Translation Batch
## Root-by-root breakdown, reasoning, and proposed literal renderings for review

**Surah:** Al-Fatiha (The Opening) — 7 Ayat
**Status:** Draft for review — not yet finalised
**Method:** Each Ayah broken into roots, mapped against the 345-root semantic lexicon, with a literal/root-faithful rendering proposed. Final wording is yours to confirm or edit.

## Ayah 1:1 — الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| الْحَمْدُ | ح-م-د | praise, commend, glorify, laud, extol, thanksgiving |
| لِلَّهِ | أ-ل-ه | Allah, God, deity, divinity, worship |
| رَبِّ | ر-ب-ب | lord, sustainer, nurturer, master, cherisher |
| الْعَالَمِينَ | ع-ل-م | worlds, realms, universe, creation |

### Reasoning

الْحَمْدُ carries the definite article (ال), making it "the praise" — a complete, all-encompassing praise rather than praise in a generic sense. لِلَّهِ combines the prefix ل (denoting "for" or "belonging to") with الله, marking the praise as specifically due to Allah. رَبِّ from ر-ب-ب carries the sense of one who sustains and nurtures — broader than the English "lord" alone suggests. الْعَالَمِينَ is plural (ينَ ending) — "worlds" in the plural is the most root-faithful rendering; a singular "world" or an abstract term like "creation" departs from this plurality.

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **All Hamd belongs to ALLAH, Rabb of al-Alamin.**

## Ayah 1:2 — الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| الرَّحْمَٰنِ | ر-ح-م | mercy, merciful, compassion, compassionate, rahman, rahim, gracious, beneficent |
| الرَّحِيمِ | ر-ح-م | mercy, merciful, compassion, compassionate, rahman, rahim, gracious, beneficent |

### Reasoning

Both words share the root ر-ح-م but take different morphological patterns. الرَّحْمَٰنِ (Ar-Rahman) follows the فَعْلَان pattern, which in Arabic intensifier morphology denotes an attribute that is all-encompassing and inherent — mercy as a defining, permanent quality. الرَّحِيمِ (Ar-Rahim) follows the فَعِيل pattern, which denotes an attribute that is recurrently active — mercy expressed continually in action. Since English has no equivalent morphological distinction, most translators render both with "merciful" variants, differing mainly in intensifier word choice ("Entirely" vs "Especially", "Lord of Mercy" vs "Giver of Mercy"). A literal rendering preserves the shared root while gesturing at the two patterns.

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **Al-Rahman, Al-Rahim.**

## Ayah 1:3 — مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| مَالِكِ | م-ل-ك | king, owner, possessor, sovereign, malik, dominion, master |
| يَوْمِ | ي-و-م | day, time, yawm, period, era |
| الدِّينِ | د-ي-ن | recompense, judgment, religion, din, debt, way, retribution |

### Reasoning

مَالِكِ from م-ل-ك most directly denotes "owner" or "possessor" — one who holds something as property. This is distinct from the related root م-ل-ك used for "king" in some forms, though both relate to dominion. "Owner" is the most root-faithful rendering, preserving the sense of possession over "Sovereign" (which leans toward the kingship sense) or "Master" (which is broader still). يَوْمِ الدِّينِ — الدِّينِ has a wide semantic range: recompense, judgment, religion, and debt are all attested meanings of د-ي-ن. In the eschatological context of "the Day of ___", "Recompense" and "Judgment" are both well-attested; "Recompense" carries the additional sense of repayment/requital present in the root's "debt" meaning, making it slightly more semantically complete.

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **Malik of Yawm al-Din.**

## Ayah 1:4 — إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| إِيَّاكَ | — | (pronoun particle: "You" — object of address, no root) |
| نَعْبُدُ | ع-ب-د | worship, servant, slave, abd, ibada, serve, devotion, obey |
| وَإِيَّاكَ | — | (pronoun particle, repeated for emphasis) |
| نَسْتَعِينُ | ع-و-ن | help, awn, aid, assist, support, seek help |

### Reasoning

The grammatical structure here is significant: إِيَّاكَ ("You" — the separate object pronoun) is placed *before* the verb نَعْبُدُ ("we worship"), which in Arabic syntax creates emphasis — "You [and only You] we worship." This fronting is a deliberate rhetorical structure (taqdim) that most English translations render with "It is You we worship" or simply preserve through word order as "You we worship." نَعْبُدُ from ع-ب-د carries the core sense of worship/service/devotion. نَسْتَعِينُ is the Form X (استفعال) verb from ع-و-ن, meaning "we seek help/assistance" — the است prefix specifically denotes seeking or requesting, so "we seek help" is more root-faithful than a plain "we ask for help" which loses the reflexive-seeking nuance.

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **You alone we do Ibadah to, and You alone we seek Istianah from**

## Ayah 1:5 — اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| اهْدِنَا | ه-د-ي | guide, guidance, huda, direct, hidaya, right path, lead |
| الصِّرَاطَ | ص-ر-ط | path, way, sirat, road, straight, route, course |
| الْمُسْتَقِيمَ | ق-و-م | stand, establish, sustain, qayyum, upright, straight |

### Reasoning

اهْدِنَا is an imperative verb from ه-د-ي with the attached object pronoun نَا ("us") — "guide us." الصِّرَاطَ from ص-ر-ط already inherently means "path/way" — note that الصِّرَاطَ itself contains the notion of a clear, traversable road. الْمُسْتَقِيمَ is the Form X (استفعال) active participle from ق-و-م ("to stand, be upright"), meaning "the one that stands straight/upright" — i.e., "the straight one." Combined, الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ is literally "the path, the straight one" — English naturally renders this as "the straight path."

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **Guide us to the Sirat al-Mustaqim —**

## Ayah 1:6 — صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| صِرَاطَ | ص-ر-ط | path, way, sirat, road, straight, route, course |
| الَّذِينَ | — | (relative pronoun: "those who" — no root) |
| أَنْعَمْتَ | ن-ع-م | blessing, favor, bounty, nima, grace, bliss, pleasure |
| عَلَيْهِمْ | — | (preposition + pronoun: "upon them" — no root) |

### Reasoning

This Ayah continues directly from 1:5, in apposition — صِرَاطَ here restates "the path of" (without the definite article, as it's in a possessive/genitive construction — إضافة). الَّذِينَ is the plural relative pronoun "those who." أَنْعَمْتَ is the Form IV (أفعال) verb from ن-ع-م, meaning "You bestowed favor/blessing" — the أَ prefix is causative, "to cause [someone] to have نِعْمَة (blessing)." عَلَيْهِمْ is "upon them." The full phrase is literally "the path of those whom You have favoured/blessed."

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **The Sirat of those upon whom You bestowed Niamah —**

## Ayah 1:7 — غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

### Root breakdown

| Arabic word | Root | Semantic field |
|---|---|---|
| غَيْرِ | غ-ي-ر | other, ghayr, different, besides, except, not |
| الْمَغْضُوبِ | غ-ض-ب | anger, wrath, ghadab, furious, rage, indignation |
| عَلَيْهِمْ | — | (preposition + pronoun: "upon them" — no root) |
| وَلَا | — | (conjunction + negation: "and not" — no root) |
| الضَّالِّينَ | ض-ل-ل | astray, misguidance, dalal, stray, lost, error, deviate |

### Reasoning

This Ayah completes the relative clause begun in 1:6, continuing the description of "the path." غَيْرِ from غ-ي-ر means "not/other than/besides" — here functioning as a negation within the genitive construction. الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ is a passive participle (مَفْعُول) from غ-ض-ب — "those upon whom anger [has fallen]" — the passive construction is significant: it describes those who *have incurred* wrath, without specifying the agent, which most translations preserve with "those who have incurred [Your] wrath" or similar. الضَّالِّينَ is the active participle plural from ض-ل-ل — "those who are astray/those who have strayed." The وَلَا ("and not") sets up the second category in parallel with the first.

### Proposed LearnQuran rendering

> **Not those upon whom Ghadab incurred, nor al-Dallin.**

## Notes for your review

A few places where I made an explicit choice that you may want to revisit:

**1:2** — "Ever-Merciful" for الرَّحِيمِ is an attempt to gesture at the فَعِيل (recurrent action) pattern, but it's a less common English phrasing. Alternatives: "the Especially Merciful" (Sahih's choice), "the Compassionate."

**1:3** — "Recompense" vs "Judgment" for الدِّينِ — both are root-faithful; "Recompense" leans toward the requital/repayment sense, "Judgment" toward the legal-verdict sense.

**1:4** — The bracketed "[alone]" reflects the emphatic fronting (taqdim) in Arabic but isn't a literal word in the text. You may prefer to remove the brackets, use italics, or render the emphasis differently (e.g., "It is You we worship").

**1:6/1:7** — "[Your]" in 1:7 is implied by context (continuing address to Allah from the surah) but not an explicit word in this Ayah. Same bracket consideration as 1:4.
