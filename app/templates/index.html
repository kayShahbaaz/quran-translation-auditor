Sahih International  |  صحيح انترناشيونال Published by Saheeh International (1997). Known for its precise, formal-equivalence English rendering. Widely used in academic and Islamic studies contexts. Tends toward literal accuracy over literary flow. Abdel Haleem  |  عبد الحليم By Muhammad Abdel Haleem (Oxford University Press, 2004). Prioritises natural, readable English. A literary translation that sometimes paraphrases for clarity, occasionally at the cost of root-level precision. Mustafa Khattab  |  مصطفى الخطاب The Clear Quran by Dr. Mustafa Khattab (2015). Balances literal accuracy with accessible modern English. Uses "Allah" throughout. Widely praised for clarity and fidelity to the Arabic. Fadel Soliman / Bridges  |  فاضل سليمان Bridges translation by Fadel Soliman. A precise, root-faithful rendering that stays very close to the Arabic grammatical structure. Uses bracketed additions to distinguish added English words from the Arabic source. Ala Hazrat / A.F. Qadri  |  كنز الإيمان Kanzul Iman — translated by Imam Ahmad Raza Khan (Ala Hazrat, 1910), rendered into English by Prof. A.F. Qadri. A classical tradition translation engrossed in love with rich honorific language for prophetic references.
